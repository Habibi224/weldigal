Who am I?

Fascinated by the way electronic systems bring technologies to life, I am Boubacar Diallo. Here is the journey that led me to specialize in electronics and embedded systems. After obtaining a scientific high school diploma in The Gambia, with a specialization in Physics, Biology, and Mathematics, I started my first year of a BTS program in signaling, energy, and telecommunications at the Institut Supérieur d’Enseignement Professionnel de Thiès (ISEPTH) in Senegal. However, I quickly noticed a significant gap between my personal aspirations and the content of the program, which was more oriented toward railway and air transport management than toward the technical aspects of engineering. This realization led me to redirect my academic path and join, with determination, the Bachelor of Technology in Electrical Engineering and Industrial Computing (GEII) at the IUT of Angoulême, even if it meant starting a year of studies again.

Currently in my third year, I naturally chose the “Electronics and Embedded Systems” track, which perfectly matches my interests. There, I mainly learn about electronic system design, embedded programming, and the integration of sensors and actuators in various technical environments. As part of this program, I completed an internship at Black Swan Technology, where I was able to put into practice, in a concrete and comprehensive way, all the skills I acquired during my studies.

About the Degree

What is the BUT GEII?

The Bachelor of Technology in Electrical Engineering and Industrial Computing (BUT GEII) is a three-year university degree delivered by Institutes of Technology (IUT). It trains intermediate-level engineers capable of working on complex electrical, electronic, and computer systems, whether in the design phase or in operation, maintenance, or continuous improvement.

This versatile and career-oriented program is built on a solid technical foundation in electricity, electronics, automation, and industrial computing. It allows students to develop both theoretical and practical skills to design, program, integrate, and maintain modern industrial systems. The BUT GEII therefore addresses major challenges related to the industry of the future, smart grids, the energy transition, and the development of connected and embedded systems, which are now widely present in industrial and technological sectors.

The BUT GEII develops essential professional skills that can be directly applied in industrial and technological environments. Students are trained in the design, programming, integration, and maintenance of electrical and electronic systems, whether fixed or embedded, while taking into account performance, reliability, and safety constraints. The program also provides expertise in automation and industrial process control through the use of programmable logic controllers, industrial networks, and human-machine interfaces (HMI) used to supervise, control, and optimize installations. Particular emphasis is placed on the development of communicating and autonomous embedded systems integrating microcontrollers, sensors, actuators, and wired or wireless communication protocols. These skills are essential to meet current needs related to the Internet of Things (IoT), robotics, and intelligent systems. From the second year onward, the program offers progressive specialization through different tracks, including:

BUT2 GEII

Electronics and Embedded Systems (ESE) focused on the design of autonomous and communicating electronic devices. ,

Electrical Engineering and Energy Management (EME), focused on the production, conversion, and management of electrical energy.

Automation and Industrial Computing (AII) oriented toward robotics, automation, and Industry 4.0. ,

The BUT GEII stands out for its practical and career-oriented approach, built around numerous practical labs, supervised projects, and learning and assessment situations (SAÉ), which place students in conditions close to the professional world. The program also includes 22 to 26 weeks of internship in companies, with the possibility of completing the degree through apprenticeship, allowing a progressive immersion in the industrial environment. This educational structure allows students to apply the skills acquired each year, develop real technical autonomy, and build the ability to work in teams and manage projects. The BUT GEII therefore prepares students both for direct entry into the workforce and for further studies up to a master’s degree level (bac +5).
